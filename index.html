About Me

Hey I'm Mason. I like to solve hard earth and planetary science problems using machine learning. I believe that our relationship with the planet will be changed by machine learning and allow us to navigate the chaotic nature of the earth. If humanity is to thrive on on earth, we need a full understanding of its dynamical process and how to deeply coexist in tandem with them.

I love hard and seemingly impossible problems. I was an initial attack wildland firefighter during the most intense wildfire season in the US, where I spent months on the line. That summer I co-founded Pluto an event venue in Los Angeles. At Brown and NASA much of my research surrounded wildfire application and then more broadly geospatial machine learning problemsthat can be solved with large remote sensing datasets. Earth Science is fascinating to me becuase of the diffuclity: a partially observable, chaotic, and poorly understood system that is tightly coupled with multi-scale physics at each level.

To understand, solve, and control these complex process, I think we need a deep convergence of our knowledge (physics), our data (remote sensing), and our modeling tools (ML/DL) to be able to make probabilistic predictions and control systems that are robust and scalable.

Other cool things: I'm an avid big wall climber, Jiu Jitsu practitioner, and DJ.
